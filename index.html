Core Security Modules

Five Modules. One Complete Identity Governance Platform.

DeskSentinel AI combines pre-fulfilment risk scoring, AI clarification of coercive chat, automated refusal summary intent generation, verification-state compliance graphing, and federated threat benchmarking all in one unified SaaS platform built specifically for UK SMEs, MSPs, and professional service firms.

Module 01

Pre-Fulfilment Identity-Risk Scoring Engine

Leverages ML to fingerprint behavioural baselines of normal user requests, scoring live interactions for social-engineering indicators urgency tone, executive impersonation, MFA fatigue before sensitive actions are permitted.

Unlike static rule-based triggers, this engine calculates a dynamic risk score for every identity-sensitive request (e.g., MFA reset or password change) before execution. It synthesises ticket content, requester behavioural baselines, and historical workflow data to identify anomalies in real-time, blocking unauthorised requests before the attack surface can be exploited. Specific Scattered Spider vishing tactics, urgency tone, and MFA fatigue triggers are automatically detected and flagged.
Module 02

AI Clarification & Refusal Summary Intent Engine

Automatically detects the true intent behind coercive or manipulative requests translating unstructured helpdesk chat into real-time operational risk conditions with actionable, auto-generated refusal summaries for agents.

When a threat actor submits a complex or coercive support request, the platform automatically clarifies the true intent, properly gauges the request complexity, and decomposes it into a structured, multi-step verification workflow without manual agent guesswork. The system identifies precisely where a security workflow breaks down and provides actionable refusal summaries, enabling agents to safely and professionally deny fraudulent requests while maintaining strict NCSC and GDPR compliance at all times.
Module 03

Verification-State Compliance Graph

Continuously evaluates whether sufficient verified evidence exists to safely authorise the requested identity change identifying exactly where a workflow breaks down before sensitive actions are executed.

This proprietary capability maps the entire identity verification chain as a live trust-state model. The graph identifies precisely where compliance breaks bypassed manager approvals, lack of registered-device confirmation, or procedural abuse and provides agents with the structured evidence they need to refuse safely. Every node in the verification graph is linked directly to the identity-security consequence of the requested action, ensuring no step can be skipped under pressure.
Module 04

Forensic Evidence Logger & Compliance Reporting

Generates audit-ready reports connecting DeskSentinel AI interventions to prevented financial loss and mitigated breach risks ensuring full ICO, NCSC Zero Trust, and UK GDPR compliance with forensic precision.

The Evidence Logger produces detailed reports connecting individual DeskSentinel AI interventions to prevented financial loss and mitigated breach risks, providing business owners with the same strategic "identity risk" intelligence used by multinational enterprises. Every intervention, clarification, and refusal is logged with high-fidelity evidence to meet strict NCSC and ICO compliance standards generating audit-ready forensic trails for every identity-sensitive change automatically.
Module 05

Community Security Network (CSN) Federated Threat Benchmarking

Federated learning architecture enabling sharing of anonymised manipulation indicators across UK firms so every SME benefits from collective regional threat intelligence without exposing private corporate data.

The CSN uses federated learning to enable sharing of anonymous manipulation indicators and social-engineering attack patterns across UK service firms. Each SME leverages regional threat intelligence without revealing private corporate data. Social-engineering trends from successful account-takeover attempts against London-based MSPs can instantly improve pre-fulfilment defences of small professional firms in Manchester creating a collective intelligence ecosystem that new cybersecurity players would have to build from scratch over years of operation.
Module 06

ITSM-Agnostic Integration Suite

Plug-and-play API platform built to overlay dominant UK business tools ServiceNow, Jira Service Management, Microsoft Entra ID, Okta minimising onboarding friction without vendor lock-in.

DeskSentinel AI sits as a governance layer above existing ITSM platforms and IAM tools, ensuring businesses aren't locked into a single provider as their technology stack evolves. The unified integration suite reduces onboarding time from weeks to hours once initial integrations are established at a pilot firm. Once the first ServiceNow and Jira integrations are stabilised, the configuration template is standardised making the onboarding cost of subsequent professional support teams very low across all plan tiers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You Need to Know About DeskSentinel AI

What exactly is the "Service-Desk Attack Surface" that DeskSentinel AI addresses?

+
The "Service-Desk Attack Surface" is the critical operational gap between technical security adoption and human-mediated operational vulnerability. While 80%+ of UK businesses have adopted Zero Trust, threat actors using sophisticated techniques like vishing (voice phishing) and MFA bypass, exemplified by the Scattered Spider group manipulate helpdesk agents into authorising unauthorised identity changes. DeskSentinel AI bridges this gap by intercepting these coercive interactions before any breach occurs, using AI clarification to decode manipulative requests and pre-fulfilment governance to block unauthorised actions before they are executed.

How does DeskSentinel AI integrate with our existing IT systems?

+
DeskSentinel AI is built with an "ITSM-Agnostic" philosophy our plug-and-play API platform connects seamlessly with dominant UK business tools including ServiceNow, Jira Service Management, Microsoft Entra ID, and Okta. This minimises onboarding friction and creates deep integration without complex data migration or infrastructure lock-in. Public APIs enable connections to the UK's dominant identity and professional toolsets, reducing onboarding time from weeks to hours once initial integrations are established at a pilot firm. The platform sits as a governance layer above your existing tools not a replacement for them.

What is "Procedural Equity" and why is it important for UK SMEs?

+
Procedural Equity is the proprietary framework by which DeskSentinel AI captures, standardises, and permanently encodes expert-level security verification logic as an organisational asset. Unlike static procedural documents kept by individual senior staff, DeskSentinel AI allows firms to own their "Identity Verification Logic" indefinitely. This means that even when senior IT staff leave, the expert-level compliance rules remain embedded in the platform instantly accessible to junior agents. It eliminates the "security loss" caused by high staff turnover in UK service desks and ensures consistent, defensible identity decisions across every agent and shift.

How does the platform help with UK regulatory compliance (NCSC, ICO, GDPR)?

+
DeskSentinel AI is strategically designed to align with the UK's evolving AI and cybersecurity regulatory landscape, specifically the NCSC's Zero Trust guidelines and the ICO's requirements on data privacy and identity verification. The platform enables businesses to track, measure, and audit every identity-verification interaction with high-fidelity logs and verifiable procedural chains via the Forensic Evidence Logger. By utilising AI Clarification capabilities, firms can detect manipulative social-engineering risks and automatically generate structured Refusal Summary Intents before unauthorised account takeovers occur providing a robust, audit-ready trail for regulatory enquiries and ICO compliance audits.

Is sensitive helpdesk data secure? How does the Community Security Network (CSN) work?

+
DeskSentinel AI maintains strict adherence to UK GDPR, ICO data protection guidance, and NCSC Zero Trust guidelines. The Community Security Network (CSN) uses federated learning architecture enabling anonymised "manipulation indicators" from UK service firms to continuously improve predictive models without sharing sensitive corporate or client data. Helpdesk interaction data is processed by secure cloud-native identity nodes with end-to-end encryption, following UK Cyber Essentials standards. Privacy-by-Design and Ethics-by-Design principles are applied throughout the platform architecture from day one, and all data entering the CSN is fully anonymised before sharing.
